FAQ

Answers to common questions about therapy at Clear Water Wellness.

Getting Started

You can request an appointment by filling out our online request form. You'll be prompted to enter basic contact information, a brief description of why you're seeking counseling, and your preferred availability. We'll contact you within the week to discuss scheduling, your assigned therapist, and payment.

We provide services for children, adolescents, adults, and geriatric populations. Clients must be 5 years of age or older.

Your first session is an opportunity for you and your therapist to get to know each other. You'll discuss what brings you to therapy, your goals, and any relevant history. It's completely normal to feel nervous—our therapists work hard to create a warm, welcoming environment. Visit our What to Expect page for a detailed breakdown.

Once your first appointment is scheduled, you'll receive a "Welcome" email from Clear Water Wellness Client Portal. Click the link to sign in and complete your forms online before your first visit. The forms are easy to access and complete through our HIPAA-compliant Simple Practice system.

Insurance & Payment

Clear Water Wellness is an out-of-network provider, which means we are not contracted with insurance companies. Payment is due at the time of service. However, if you have out-of-network benefits, you may be able to get 40-80% reimbursed. Visit our Rates & Insurance page to learn more and check your benefits.

We accept credit cards, debit cards, and HSA/FSA cards. Payment is due at the time of service.

A superbill is an itemized receipt that contains all the information your insurance company needs to process a reimbursement claim. We provide superbills upon request. You submit it to your insurance company, and they reimburse you directly based on your out-of-network benefits.

Clinical intern sessions are available at reduced rates. Working with a supervised clinical intern can be a great option for quality care at a lower cost. Ask us about availability when you schedule.

Appointments

Session frequency depends on your individual needs and goals. Some clients attend weekly, others every two weeks. Your therapist will work with you to determine what's best for your situation. We recommend scheduling recurring appointments to secure your preferred time slot.

Yes! If you're unable to attend in person, you can attend virtually. Just let your therapist know, and they'll send you a secure video link for your appointment. Telehealth is available for all clients.

We ask that you cancel appointments at least 24 hours in advance to avoid a cancellation fee. You can cancel online through your Client Portal. You'll receive a text reminder 24 hours before each appointment.

Log in to your Client Portal to request new appointments or cancel existing ones. Your Client Portal also allows you to view your therapist's availability, complete forms, and more.

Services

Animal Assisted Therapy (AAT) incorporates a certified therapy animal into sessions. Our therapy dog Bowie (a Newfypoo) accompanies Renee Stout, LCSW during sessions, providing comfort, emotional safety, and connection. Many clients find the presence of a therapy dog helps them feel more relaxed and open.

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is an evidence-based therapy for processing trauma and PTSD. It uses bilateral stimulation (like eye movements) to help the brain reprocess traumatic memories, making them less emotionally overwhelming. Morgan Ogozalek, LPC, CCTP provides EMDR therapy at our practice.

Yes, we offer thorough ADHD assessments using DSM-5TR criteria, multi-source input, and narrative reports. These evaluations can support 504/IEP plans or medication management discussions with your healthcare provider.

We offer three free support groups: Survivors of Suicide (1st & 3rd Tuesday), Grief Support (2nd Tuesday), and Pregnancy & Infant Loss (2nd Thursday). All groups are free, open to the public, and require no registration. Visit our Support Groups page for details.
