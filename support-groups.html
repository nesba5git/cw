Survivors of Suicide
Support Group

If you have lost a loved one by suicide, the pain, shock, and confusion can seem unbearable—you are not alone. Suicide loss survivors share a singular bond. While each situation is unique, survivors can experience similar feelings, questions, and complex grief.

This group's mission is to provide a safe place for survivors to share their experience (thoughts and feelings). Support groups are designed to help a group of people that share similar experiences. It is through the sharing of our stories that we can better understand and heal while riding the roller coaster that is grief.

What to Expect

A safe space
to be heard

In this group you will not be pressured to talk, share your story, or grief. Therapeutic information is offered to survivors to assist in healthy coping and healing skills.

Group often provides an opportunity for survivors to learn from the struggles and victories of others in the group. The suicide bereavement process is difficult for people to "navigate" making the topic taboo or even stigmatized.

If you choose to discuss your loss, speaking can allow other group members to offer insight and advice based on their own unique experience.

Important Details

No Pressure

You will never be pressured to talk, share your story, or express your grief. Participate at your own comfort level—listening is always welcome.

Therapeutic Tools

Therapeutic information is offered to help survivors develop healthy coping and healing skills for their grief journey.
